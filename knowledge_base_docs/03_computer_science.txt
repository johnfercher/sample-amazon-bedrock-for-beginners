Greenfield University — Computer Science Department FAQ

Q: Where is the Computer Science department located?
A: The Computer Science department is in the Turing Engineering Building, 3rd floor, rooms 300-320. The main office is room 301.

Q: What degrees does the CS department offer?
A: We offer a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science, a Minor in Computer Science, and a combined BS/MS five-year program.

Q: What programming languages are taught in the introductory courses?
A: CS 101 (Intro to Programming) uses Python. CS 201 (Data Structures) uses Java. Upper-level electives cover C++, JavaScript, Rust, and others depending on the course.

Q: Do I need prior programming experience to major in CS?
A: No. CS 101 assumes no prior experience. Students with AP Computer Science credit or equivalent may place directly into CS 201.

Q: Who is the department chair?
A: Dr. Maria Chen is the current department chair. Her office is Turing 305 and her office hours are Tuesdays and Thursdays from 2:00 to 4:00 PM.

Q: Is there a CS tutoring center?
A: Yes. The CS Help Desk is in Turing 110, open Monday through Thursday from 6:00 to 10:00 PM. Peer tutors can help with courses from CS 101 through CS 350. No appointment needed.
