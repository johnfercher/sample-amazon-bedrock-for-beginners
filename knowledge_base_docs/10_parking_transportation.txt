Greenfield University — Parking and Transportation FAQ

Q: Do I need a parking permit?
A: Yes. All students, faculty, and staff who park on campus must display a valid parking permit. Permits are purchased online through the Transportation Office portal.

Q: How much does a parking permit cost?
A: Student permits are $250 per semester for commuter lots and $400 per semester for residential lots. A discounted annual rate is available if purchased before August 1.

Q: Is there a campus shuttle?
A: Yes. The Greenfield Shuttle runs three routes connecting campus to downtown, the train station, and the nearby shopping district. Shuttles run every 15 minutes from 7:00 AM to 11:00 PM on weekdays and every 30 minutes on weekends.

Q: Can first-year students bring a car?
A: First-year students living on campus are not permitted to have a car during their first semester. Starting in the spring semester, first-years may apply for a commuter lot permit.

Q: Where do I park for a campus visit?
A: Visitors may park in the Welcome Center lot off Main Street. Parking is free for up to 4 hours with a visitor pass from the Admissions Office.

Q: Are there bike racks and bike-share options?
A: Yes. Bike racks are located outside every academic building and residence hall. Greenfield also partners with GreenCycle bike-share, offering students free 30-minute rides with a valid student ID.
