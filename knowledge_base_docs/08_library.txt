Greenfield University — Library Services FAQ

Q: What are the library hours?
A: The Morrison Library is open Monday through Thursday 7:00 AM to midnight, Friday 7:00 AM to 9:00 PM, Saturday 10:00 AM to 9:00 PM, and Sunday 10:00 AM to midnight. Extended hours are available during finals week.

Q: How do I borrow books?
A: Bring your Greenfield student ID to the circulation desk. Undergraduates may borrow up to 20 items at a time. Loan periods are 21 days for books and 3 days for DVDs and reserve materials.

Q: Does the library have study rooms?
A: Yes. There are 15 group study rooms on the 2nd and 3rd floors, reservable online in 2-hour blocks. Individual study carrels are available on the 4th floor on a first-come, first-served basis.

Q: Can I access research databases from off campus?
A: Yes. Log in through the library website using your Greenfield credentials to access all subscribed databases, e-journals, and e-books from anywhere.

Q: Does the library offer printing and scanning?
A: Yes. Black-and-white printing is $0.05 per page and color printing is $0.25 per page. Scanning is free. Printers are located on every floor. Print credits can be added at the circulation desk or online.

Q: Is there a laptop lending program?
A: Yes. The library has 30 laptops available for 4-hour in-library use. Semester-long laptop loans are available through the IT Help Desk for students who demonstrate financial need.
