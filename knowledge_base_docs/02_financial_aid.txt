Greenfield University — Financial Aid FAQ

Q: How do I apply for financial aid?
A: Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at studentaid.gov. Greenfield's school code is 003456. The priority deadline is March 1 each year.

Q: What types of financial aid are available?
A: Greenfield offers federal grants (Pell Grant, SEOG), institutional scholarships, work-study positions, and federal student loans (Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized). Merit scholarships are awarded automatically based on your admission application.

Q: When will I receive my financial aid award letter?
A: Award letters are sent within 2-3 weeks of your FAFSA being processed. Most students receive their letters by mid-April for the following academic year.

Q: Can I appeal my financial aid package?
A: Yes. If your family has experienced a significant change in financial circumstances, you may submit a Financial Aid Appeal Form along with supporting documentation to the Financial Aid Office in Whitman Hall, Room 104.

Q: How is work-study different from a regular campus job?
A: Work-study is a federally funded program. Your earnings are based on financial need and do not count against your aid package. Regular campus jobs are paid from university funds and are open to all students regardless of need.

Q: What GPA do I need to keep my scholarship?
A: Most institutional scholarships require a cumulative GPA of 3.0 or higher, reviewed at the end of each spring semester. Students who fall below may be placed on a one-semester probation period.
