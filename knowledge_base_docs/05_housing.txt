Greenfield University — Housing and Residence Life FAQ

Q: Is on-campus housing guaranteed?
A: On-campus housing is guaranteed for all first-year and second-year students. Upperclassmen may apply through the housing lottery, which opens each February.

Q: What residence halls are available for first-year students?
A: First-year students are assigned to one of three halls: Maple Hall, Cedar Hall, or Birch Hall. All are traditional-style with double rooms and shared bathrooms by floor.

Q: Can I request a specific roommate?
A: Yes. Both students must submit mutual roommate requests through the housing portal by June 1. If no request is made, roommates are matched based on the housing questionnaire.

Q: What is included in the room?
A: Each room comes with a twin XL bed, desk, desk chair, dresser, closet or wardrobe, and mini-blinds. Students should bring their own bedding, towels, and personal items.

Q: How much does on-campus housing cost?
A: For the 2026-2027 academic year, the standard double room rate is $6,200 per semester. Single rooms are $7,800 per semester. Rates include utilities, Wi-Fi, and laundry access.

Q: Are there quiet floors or themed housing options?
A: Yes. Each first-year hall has a designated quiet floor. Themed communities include the STEM Living-Learning Community in Cedar Hall and the Global Perspectives floor in Birch Hall.
