Greenfield University — Course Registration FAQ

Q: How do I register for classes?
A: Log in to the student portal at my.greenfield.edu and navigate to "Course Registration." Registration opens based on class standing: seniors register first, followed by juniors, sophomores, and first-years.

Q: When does registration open?
A: Fall 2026 registration opens in early April. Exact dates by class standing are published on the Registrar's website by March 15.

Q: What do I do if a class is full?
A: You can add yourself to the waitlist through the student portal. If a seat opens, you will be notified by email and have 24 hours to confirm your enrollment. You may also contact the instructor to request an override.

Q: How many credits can I take per semester?
A: A standard full-time course load is 12 to 18 credits. Students wishing to take more than 18 credits must receive approval from their academic advisor and the Dean of Students.

Q: Can I take courses at other universities?
A: Greenfield participates in a consortium agreement with three nearby institutions. Students may cross-register for up to two courses per semester. Pre-approval from your advisor is required.

Q: How do I change my major?
A: Submit a Change of Major form through the student portal. You will need approval from your current advisor and the department chair of your new major. Most changes are processed within one week.
