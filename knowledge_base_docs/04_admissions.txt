Greenfield University — Admissions FAQ

Q: What is the application deadline?
A: Early Decision deadline is November 1. Regular Decision deadline is January 15. Transfer applications are due April 1 for fall entry and November 1 for spring entry.

Q: Is the SAT or ACT required?
A: Greenfield is test-optional for the 2026-2027 admissions cycle. Students may submit SAT or ACT scores if they feel the scores reflect their abilities, but they are not required.

Q: What is the average GPA of admitted students?
A: The middle 50% GPA range for the Fall 2025 entering class was 3.5 to 3.9 on a 4.0 scale.

Q: How do I schedule a campus tour?
A: Campus tours are offered Monday through Friday at 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM, and Saturdays at 10:00 AM. Register online at greenfield.edu/visit or call the Admissions Office at (555) 123-4567.

Q: Does Greenfield accept AP and IB credits?
A: Yes. Greenfield awards credit for AP exam scores of 4 or 5 and IB Higher Level scores of 5 or above. A full list of accepted exams and equivalent courses is available on the Registrar's website.

Q: What is the acceptance rate?
A: Greenfield's acceptance rate for Fall 2025 was approximately 42%.
